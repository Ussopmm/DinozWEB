Apatosaurus
$400

Apatosaurus, formerly known as Brontosaurus, is a herbivorous dinosaur that lived during the Late Jurassic period, approximately 150 million years ago. Here are some key features and facts about Apatosaurus: Size and Appearance: Apatosaurus was a colossal dinosaur, reaching lengths of up to 75 feet (23 meters) and weighing around 20 to 35 tons. It had a long neck, a small head with a small brain, a massive body, a long tail, and column-like legs. Herbivorous Diet: Apatosaurus was a plant-eating dinosaur, feeding on vegetation such as ferns, conifers, and other plants. Its teeth were adapted for stripping leaves and twigs from plants, but it is believed to have swallowed food without much chewing, relying on its massive gut for digestion.

Parasaurolophus
$99.99

Parasaurolophus is a herbivorous dinosaur that lived during the Late Cretaceous period, approximately 76 to 73 million years ago. Here are some key features and facts about Parasaurolophus: Crested Head: One of the most distinctive features of Parasaurolophus is its elaborate crest on top of its head. The crest was a long, curved, hollow tube that extended backward from the skull. It is believed to have been used for communication, producing various sounds and possibly enhancing the dinosaur's sense of smell. Size and Appearance: Parasaurolophus was a large dinosaur, measuring around 33 to 40 feet (10 to 12 meters) in length and weighing approximately 2 to 3.5 tons. It had a long, slender body, a long neck, a small head, and a long tail. Herbivorous Diet: Parasaurolophus was a plant-eating dinosaur that primarily fed on low-lying vegetation such as ferns and horsetails. It had a beak-like mouth and specialized teeth for cropping and chewing plant material.
